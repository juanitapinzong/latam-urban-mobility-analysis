# Urban Mobility and Economic Productivity in Latin American Cities

This project analyzes the relationship between urban mobility and economic productivity across major Latin American cities.

The analysis combines traffic congestion data from the **TomTom Traffic Index** with economic indicators from the **OECD Cities dataset** to explore how urban mobility conditions may influence economic performance.

## Project Objective

The goal of this project is to identify patterns between traffic congestion and economic productivity indicators such as:

- GDP per capita
- Unemployment rate
- Urban population
- Traffic congestion levels

Understanding these relationships can help policymakers identify cities where improving transportation infrastructure may support economic growth.

## Data Sources

Two datasets were used:

**TomTom Traffic Index**
- Traffic congestion index
- Traffic delays
- Travel time per 10 km

**OECD Cities Dataset**
- GDP per capita
- Unemployment rate
- Population
- Air pollution levels

## Analysis Process

The project includes the following steps:

1. Data loading and exploration  
2. Data cleaning and standardization  
3. Filtering cities in Latin America  
4. Aggregating traffic indicators by city  
5. Merging mobility and economic datasets  
6. Exploratory data analysis and visualization
